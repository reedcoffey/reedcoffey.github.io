It's finally time to rest! I'd been working overtime to try and finish up my contracted hours at my last position, so I haven't had much time or energy to pour into my creative works. Thankfully, the crunch is at an end and I've been able to take a breather to gather myself and some inspiration. Where better to go than to the Redwoods in Northern California? I'm still a little torn if I can refer to the region as the Pacific Northwest, but it does bear some resemblance to the place where I grew up. My partner and I drove out to Redwoods State and National Parks near Crescent City and spent some lovely time soaking up the trees and the water, so different from the Sonoran Desert. This trip was more about gathering references than putting brush to canvas, but I feel like my artistic practice has been in great need of this kind of trip.

We spent three nights in the Redwoods in awe of the towering trunks, the full height of which I can't even begin to capture the effect of on camera, so I'm not even going to try. What I was most in awe of though, was each giant's ability to support other life forms upon it. Nurse logs, moss hanging from branches, slugs sliming around, it really felt like home to me. The mosquitoes were more than happy to host, to be sure.


Spending time staring up at the branches above me was a good reminder that art doesn't have to be a race, and that it's good and necessary to take time to just absorb, rather than constantly create. Just as it takes years to build ring after ring on a tree, earlywood cradled in by latewood, creating needs times of energetic bursts and time of slow contemplation. These last few months have been the latter, and it's only making me more excited for what I'm going to come up with next.
